Civil War Battle Timeline

  • Battle of Fort Sumter

    Southern states disliked Lincoln as president, as a result: 7 states seceded starting with South Carolina. 6 more states followed creating the Confederate states of America. They requested Lincoln to surrender the United states property to them. Lincoln did not and sent supplies (not troops or weapons because he didn’t want war.) to Fort Sumter. The supply ship was sent away, so there were negotiations between the Fort Sumter commander and a Confederate General which failed. Then on April 9th president of the Confederates: President Jefferson Davis, wanted to strike Fort Sumter. The order was given on the 10th to strike and after a stalemate, of staring, confederate guns started to fire on the 12th. Casualties: none. Union: 80 troops. Confederates: 500 troops. Winner: Confederates.

    April 12, 1861

  • Battle of Philippi

    This was the first organized land battle. It was to protect the railroads from Baltimore to Ohio. Union: 3,000 troops. Confederates: 800 troops. Casualties: 30 (4 Union, 26 Confederate) Winner: Union.

    June 30, 1861

  • Battle of Ball's Bluff

    A scout mistook a line of trees for tents. When the army was sent out, they encountered Mississippi infantry. Union: 1,720 troops. Confederates: 1,709 troops. Casualties: 1,070 (921 Union, 149 Confederate) Winner: Confederates

    October 21, 1861

  • Battle of Fort Donelson

    Goal was to capture or have control over major rivers. The Fort of Donelson had control over the Tennessee River. Ulysses S. Grant surrounded the Confederates. Union: 24,531 troops. Confederates: 16,717 troops. Casualties: 17,398 (2,331 Union, 15,067 Confederate) Winner: Union

    February 11, 1862

  • Siege of Yorktown

    Major General of the Union Army,George B. Mclellan got frustrated and decided to take rebel lines over by force. Union 121,500 troops. Confederates: 35,000 troops. Casualties: 482 (182 Union, 300 Confederate) Winner: Union

    April 5, 1862

  • Battle of Shiloh

    This was a battle to protect Confederate Railroads. If Confederates lose, they will have their army split into 2. Without having a way to transport their troops. Union 65,085 troops. Confederates: 44,968 troops. Casualties: 23,741 (13,047 Union, 10,694 Confederate) Winner: Union

    April 6, 1862

  • Capture of New Orleans

    43 union ships sailed into the ports of New Orleans and the General, Mansfield Lovell, surrendered knowing it was useless to fight. 15,000 Union troops. 3,000 Confederate troops. Casualties: None Winner: Union

    April 25, 1862

  • Battle of Seven Pines

    Fight was over Chickahominy River near the Seven Pines crossroads. Confederate Gen. Joseph E. Johnston wanted to attack 2 Union Corps. Ignored one Union Corp out of commission while Union did hold their ground. 34,000 Union troops. 39,000 Confederate troops. Casualties: 13,736 (5,739 Union, 7,997 Confederate) Winner: Inconclusive

    May 31, 1862

  • Battle of Gettysburg

    General Robert E. Lee of the Confederates wanted to capitalize on recent Confederate victories. He also wanted to take the fight out of Virginia. Though Lee was chased by the Union and eventually crossed at Gettysburg. 93,921 Union troops. 71,699 Confederate troops. Casualties: 51,000 (23,000 Union, 28,000 Confederate) Winner: Union

    July 1, 1863

  • Battle of Chickamauga

    Fight was to occupy Chattanooga, from there able to spread throughout Tennessee. The Union occupied it so Braxton Bragg attacked there. 65,000 Union troops. 65,000 Confederate troops. Casualties: 34,624 (16,170 Union, 18,454 Confederate) Winner: Confederates

    September 19, 1863

  • Battle of Franklin

    When the Confederate leader John Bell Hood, had supplies cut short in recent battles. John M. Schofield was sent to finish him off, for there to be a lot less issues in Tennessee. 30,000 Union troops. 33,000 Confederate troops. Casualties: 8,587(2,326 Union, 6,261 Confederate) Winner: Union

    November 30, 1864

  • Surrender at Appomattox Court House

    General Lee’s army was cornered after his supplies were found by the Union first. When trying to move to attack, he got the Confederate Army split between the Union Army. With General Lee trapped, he negotiated the surrender at the Appomattox Court House, ending the civil war. 63,285 Union troops. 26,000 Confederate troops. Casualties: 652(152 Union, 500 Confederate) Winner: Union

    April 9, 1865
